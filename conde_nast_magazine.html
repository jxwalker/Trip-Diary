YOUR PERSONALIZED ITINERARY

We've crafted a carefully curated four-day itinerary that balances iconic landmarks with hidden gems, ensuring you experience the very best of New York City. Each day is designed to maximize your time while allowing for spontaneous discoveries and local experiences.

Day 1

Sunday, November 10
Morning: Explore Greenwich Village, visit the Flatiron Building and Madison Square Park
Afternoon: Tour the New York Public Library on Fifth Avenue, then visit the Empire State Building and Grand Central Terminal
Evening: Enjoy sunset views from Top of the Rock and explore Hudson Yards
Getting Around: Walking between Greenwich Village, Flatiron Building, and Madison Square Park is easy; subway or short taxi rides recommended between afternoon and evening spots
Estimated Cost: $50-$70 (Empire State Building and Top of the Rock tickets approx. $40 each, library and park free)

Day 2

Monday, November 11
Morning: Visit the 9/11 Memorial and One World Trade Center Observatory
Afternoon: Walk through the Financial District including Wall Street, see the Charging Bull and Oculus Centre; optional Staten Island Ferry ride for Statue of Liberty views
Evening: Dinner in Lower East Side or Chinatown
Getting Around: Use subway to reach World Trade Center station; walking recommended within Financial District; ferry from Battery Park for Staten Island trip
Estimated Cost: $35-$50 (One World Observatory approx. $38, ferry free, meals vary)

Day 3

Tuesday, November 12
Morning: Stroll through Central Park from south to mid-park, visit Bethesda Terrace and Literary Walk
Afternoon: Explore the Metropolitan Museum of Art (MET)
Evening: Experience Times Square and optionally a Broadway show
Getting Around: Walking within Central Park and to MET; subway or taxi to Times Square and Broadway theaters
Estimated Cost: $75-$150 (MET admission approx. $30, Broadway show tickets vary widely $50-$120+)

Day 4

Wednesday, November 13
Morning: Walk across Brooklyn Bridge early morning
Afternoon: Explore DUMBO neighborhood, visit Time Out Market rooftop and waterfront
Evening: Cocktails in Harlem or Upper East Side
Getting Around: Subway to Brooklyn Bridge-City Hall station; walking across bridge and around DUMBO; subway or taxi to Harlem or Upper East Side
Estimated Cost: $40-$80 (mostly free walking, drinks $15-$25 each, optional food expenses)

ESSENTIAL INFORMATION

Getting Around

New York's subway system is the most efficient way to navigate the city. Purchase a MetroCard or use contactless payment. Taxis and ride-sharing services are readily available. Walking is often the best way to experience the city's energy and discover hidden gems.

Currency & Payments

US Dollar (USD). Credit cards are widely accepted, but carry some cash for small purchases, street vendors, and tips. Most establishments accept contactless payments.

Emergency Information

911 for police, fire, and medical emergencies. 311 for non-emergency city services. Keep your hotel's address and phone number handy.

Local Customs & Tips

New Yorkers walk fast and expect the same from visitors. Stand to the right on escalators, and don't block subway doors. Tipping 18-20% is standard for restaurants and services. Be prepared for the city's energy and embrace the pace.
